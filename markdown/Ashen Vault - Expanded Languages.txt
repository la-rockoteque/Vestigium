# The Languages of the Veil ‚Äì Fragments of a Dead Tongue  

The languages of the Veil do not function like those of the mortal world. They do not evolve along structured lines, nor do they decay in predictable ways. Instead, they **fracture, warp, and shift**, influenced by **perception, planar instability, and the unknowable forces that shape the Veil itself**.  

At the root of all Veil speech lies **Umbrial**, a **dead tongue**, spoken by no living being. It exists only in ancient texts, drifting echoes, and incomprehensible inscriptions left behind by an extinct civilization. Though its structure is lost, its **influence lingers**, shaping the modern dialects spoken by Veilborn, travelers, and those who dare to cross between worlds.

# Linguistic Breakdown: Umbrial, Veilcant, and Noctalspeech

These three languages exist within the **Veil and its peripheries**, shaped by **ancient traditions, interplanar trade, and cultural necessity**. While some have written forms, others rely entirely on oral transmission, making documentation a challenge.

## The Unique Properties of Veil Speech

Unlike structured languages, the tongues of the Veil behave in **unpredictable, often supernatural ways**.  

- **Time & Distance Matter** ‚Üí A phrase spoken in one region of the Veil **may not be heard the same way elsewhere**, or may be completely unheard by those too far removed.  
- **Writing is Impossible** ‚Üí Attempts to **record Veil speech** result in **text rewriting itself**, words disappearing, or messages that change **when read by different people**.  
- **Intent Shapes Meaning** ‚Üí In Veilcant, **the same phrase spoken by two different people** may carry **completely opposite meanings**, depending on their **state of mind and location**.  
- **Speech May Linger** ‚Üí Some words, once spoken, **continue to exist independently**, drifting like echoes that **replay at unknown intervals**, sometimes in places they were never originally spoken.

\page

## Umbrial ‚Äì The Language of Forgotten Civilizations
*A dead language, lost to time. Umbrial is not spoken in everyday conversation, only found in cryptic glyphs, ancient records, and artifacts uncovered in ruins. Unlike mortal languages, Umbrial does not follow a conventional grammatical structure. Some scholars believe that certain words carry deep symbolic weight, as though they were never meant to be spoken aloud.*

### Linguistic Features
- **Logographic & Conceptual** ‚Üí Unlike phonetic languages, Umbrial conveys meaning through **symbols representing ideas rather than direct sounds**.  
- **Nonlinear Structure** ‚Üí Sentence order **does not follow a strict pattern**, meaning a phrase may be **read in multiple valid ways** depending on context.  
- **Ancient Script** ‚Üí Uses **complex, interwoven glyphs** that are often carved or embossed rather than written with ink.  
- **Layered Meaning** ‚Üí A single glyph **can represent multiple words or concepts**, requiring contextual interpretation.  

### Alphabet & Writing System
- **Glyph-Based** ‚Üí Written Umbrial consists of **blocky, carved symbols** often found on ancient ruins.  
- **No Modern Transliteration** ‚Üí Some scholars have attempted to **map Umbrial glyphs to phonetic equivalents**, but no full translation exists.  
- **Directional Reading** ‚Üí Some glyphs are read **vertically, diagonally, or spirally**, depending on inscription style.  

### Pronunciation & Speech**  
- **Rarely Spoken** ‚Üí Umbrial **lacks a standard pronunciation**, as it was historically used for **record-keeping, ritual inscriptions, and prophetic writings** rather than common speech.  
- **Complex Consonant Clusters** ‚Üí When vocalized, Umbrial **relies heavily on deep, guttural sounds**, similar to a cross between ancient Draconic and lost Elvish dialects.

## Veilcant ‚Äì The Trade Language of the Veil
*A widely spoken language among **Veilborn, Umbranauts, and interplanar smugglers**, Veilcant is a **pragmatic, adaptable tongue** used primarily for **commerce, navigation, and diplomacy**. It is a direct and functional language with a focus on **efficiency and coded subtext**.*

### Linguistic Features
